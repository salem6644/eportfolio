Risk

Risk definition, Qualitative and Quantitative risk assessments.

Risk Definition

The definition of risk evolved over the time

  • Risk is the possibility of an event where its occurrence would reduce the value of the business. Such an event is called “adverse event” (Blakley et al. 2001)
  • Risk is the net negative impact of the exercise of a vulnerability taking into account both the probability and the impact of occurrence (Stoneburner et al. 2002).
  • Risk is a state of uncertainty where some of the possibilities involve undesirable outcome, e.g., loss, injury, etc. (Hubbard 2009).
  • Risk is probable frequency and magnitude of future loss (Hietala 2014).
  • Information risk is possibility of an event where its occurrence would negatively impact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of an information system (Izuakor 2016).

Risk management is the process of identifying risk, assessing risk, and taking steps to reduce risk to an acceptable level (Stoneburner et al. 2002).

There are two ways to carry out risk assessment

  • Qualitative Risk Assessment
  • Quantitative Risk Assessment

Qualitative Risk Assessment

Qualitative risk assessment is a method used to evaluate risks by assessing their characteristics and impacts based on non-numerical data. It uses descriptive and subjective criteria to determine the severity and likelihood of risks (Olson and Desheng 2020).

Quantitative Risk Assessment

Quantitative risk assessment is a systematic approach used to quantify risks using numerical data and statistical methods. It aims to provide measurable and objective estimates of the likelihood and impact of risks. This approach is often used in fields where precise risk evaluation is critical, such as finance, engineering, healthcare, and safety management (Olson and Desheng 2020).
