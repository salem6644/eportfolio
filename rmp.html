Risk Managmet Process (RMP)

The risk management process involves systematically identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks to achieve organizational objectives.

RMP based on ISO 31000

ISO 31000 is an international standard for risk management that provides guidelines and principles for managing risk effectively within an organization. Updated in 2018, the standard emphasizes the importance of integrating risk management into all aspects of an organization's activities, particularly
focusing on the role of leadership in ensuring risk management supports governance and decision-making processes (Paul and Cadle 2020).

Risk Identification

It is the process of finding, recognizing, and describing risks. That can be done in many ways

  • Historical Data
  • Interviews
  • Workshops

Risk Analysis

It is the systematic process of understanding the nature of risks and determining their level. This involves identifying potential events that could affect objectives, analyzing the likelihood and consequences of these events, and combining these factors to assess the overall level of risk. The process helps organizations understand the extent of potential impacts and prioritize risks based on their severity.

Risk Evaluation

It involves assessing the significance of identified risks and determining whether they are acceptable based on the organization's risk criteria. This step follows risk identification and analysis, helping prioritize risks for treatment. The process combines both quantitative and qualitative approaches, using data-driven metrics and expert judgments to assess potential impacts and likelihoods. The goal is to support informed decision-making by evaluating risks in the context of the organization's objectives, risk appetite, and regulatory requirements.

Risk Treatment

It involves selecting and implementing measures to modify risk. This process includes deciding on strategies such as:

  • Avoid the risk - remove the risk completely
  • Tolerate the risk - no action is taken to reduce the risk
  • Reduce the risk - taking action to reduce the impact of the risk by preventative actions
  • Transfer the risk - where the cost of mitigation and or impact of a risk is shared between multiple stakeholders

The goal is to reduce the risk to an acceptable level aligned with the organization’s risk criteria. Effective risk treatment plans are tailored to the specific context and requirements of the organization, ensuring comprehensive risk management that supports organizational objectives and resilience​

Mitigating mechanisms

  • Physical controls e.g., CCTV
  • Procedural controls e.g., processes and procedures that staff should follow
  • Technical controls e.g., Firewall, Encryption, etc.

User Participantion in RMP

Changes in user participation can significantly impact the risk management process by enhancing the identification and mitigation of risks. Increased user involvement allows for diverse perspectives, leading to a more comprehensive understanding of potential risks and their impacts. Users can provide valuable insights into practical challenges and operational realities, enabling more accurate risk assessments. This collaboration fosters a culture of risk awareness and shared responsibility, improving the effectiveness of risk treatment strategies. Additionally, involving users in the monitoring and review stages ensures continuous feedback, allowing for timely adjustments to risk management practices. Overall, greater user participation enhances the robustness and adaptability of the risk management process (Villanueva 2023).
