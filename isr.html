Information Securtiy and Risk Managment

In information security, both qualitative and quantitative risk assessment methods are essential for identifying, analyzing, and mitigating risks.

Qualitative Risk Assessment

Qualitative methods focus on subjective evaluations and are scenario-based. They utilize expert judgment and descriptive scales to assess the impact and likelihood of risks. Common approaches include the DREAD model, which evaluates risk based on Damage potential, Reproducibility, Exploitability, Affected users, and Discoverability, and the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS), which uses metrics to describe exploitability and impact. These methods are useful for quickly assessing risks and making informed decisions without the need for extensive numerical data (Infosec 2018; Evrin 2019).

Quantitative Risk Assessment

Quantitative methods assign numerical values to risk components, allowing for precise calculations. Key variables include Exposure Factor (EF), Single Loss Expectancy (SLE), Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO), and Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE). For example, if a corporate laptop valued at $100,000 has a 30% exposure factor due to infrequent backups, and the annualized rate of theft occurrence is 0.5, the annualized loss expectancy can be calculated.
